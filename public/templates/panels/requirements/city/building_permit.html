Getting a Building Permit is a multi-step process, and is best accomplished working with a certified architect, engineer or design professional.

Typically, the process begins with an initial submittal of plans drawn up to scale. The building division takes these plans and routes them around the City for experts to check. Typically, plans are reviewed by a number of City Departments including building, fire, public works and water. You'll get separate comments back from each of these departments.

After review, you'll receive comments which you'll have to address in the next revision of the plans. Once all open issues are sorted, you'll be issued an building permit, which you'll post at your worksite.

Once you've passed final inspections on site, you'll receive a "Certificate of Occupancy" from the Building division for your commercial space, which means its safe and fully permitted for the public to utilize.

This process does have charges associated with it to capture the labor costs the City undertakes to review the plans. Rates are calculated both on the complexity of the project and the valuation of the build.

A general guideline is that a building permit will cost between 5-10% of the value of the building project that you'll undertake.
